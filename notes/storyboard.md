| Seth Rogen and Charlize Theron are in similar numbers of films (they even costarred in "Long Shot"), but Charlize Theron tends to work with new actors, as opposed to SR (e.g. Jonah Hill is in 8 of his films). _Concurrency_ is a measure of how frequently you work with the same actors. Seth Rogen having a concurrency of 0.28 means that for each of his films, he would have worked with 28% of them before. If your concurrency is low, and you work for the first time with actors more often, you will create more connections in the graph, and it'll take you fewer films to get that average distance down.                                                                                                                                                                 | Show a [scatter](https://kevin-bacon-components.vercel.app/?path=/docs/components-concurrence-vs-films-scatter--docs) plot of log(film_count) on the X, but concurrency on the Y.
